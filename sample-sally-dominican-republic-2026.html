Your Trip at a Glance

Ten days of Caribbean sun, included.

Punta Cana sits on the eastern tip of Hispaniola, where the Atlantic rolls in from the right and the Caribbean laps from the left — resulting in some of the most consistent beach conditions in the region. Temperatures in June hold steady in the low-to-mid 80s, the water is warm and clear, and the high season crowds have thinned considerably. Below are three resort options across different price points and vibes. Pick one — or tell me what's pulling you toward or away from any of them — and we'll lock it in.

Cap Cana is a separate gated community 15 minutes south of Punta Cana airport — quieter, less trafficked, and home to a marina with actual deep-sea fishing boats if that's your thing. What to Know

A few things that'll make your trip easier.

🛂

Entry Requirements

U.S. citizens need a valid passport (6 months beyond travel). No visa required. The DR charges a $10 tourist card, now included in most airfares automatically — confirm with your airline.

🌧️

June Weather

Highs in the low 80s°F. June is technically rainy season, but showers tend to be short and intense — 20 minutes, then gone. Rarely does weather cancel a beach day. It's also early hurricane season; travel insurance is worth it.

💵

Money on the Ground

All-inclusive means drinks, food, and most activities are covered. Budget $10–$20/day in USD for tips (bartenders, room attendants), plus any off-resort excursions or spa treatments.

✈️

Getting There

Punta Cana Airport (PUJ) has direct service from most major U.S. hubs. Flight time from the East Coast is roughly 3–4 hours. Flights are not included in this quote — ask me about current pricing.
